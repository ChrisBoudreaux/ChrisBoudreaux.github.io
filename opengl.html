OpenGL Flocking Behavior Project

This is a project that I worked on for a course on computer graphics at University of Louisiana at Lafayette.

It was developed incrementally over the course of one semester, going from learning how to set up a triangle strip to developing a scene graph to maintain the positions of objects within a complex scene. Here, the result of the final assignment is a textured planet with a textured sky sphere. Flying around the planet are from 50 to 100 agents adhering to the flocking behavior principles described at Wikipedia with some adjustments and addtional parameters added to account for prioritizing influences and avoiding obstacles (red spehres). Additionally, it can be viewed in 3D, but was originally configured for a 46 inch 3DTV, so things may look strange or scaled improperly on a smaller monitor.
